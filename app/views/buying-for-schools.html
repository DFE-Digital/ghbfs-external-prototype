Get free and impartial help from procurement specialists

Use Get help buying for schools for advice, help and support from our team of DfE procurement specialists on buying goods and services.

Our procurement specialists can give advice at any stage of your procurement process.

This service applies to all state funded schools in England.

Use self-guided tools

You can use our tools at any time to help you at specific stages of your procurement. It's easier to use these tools if you're already familiar with the procurement process.

Use a DfE recommended buying solution

We recommend using a framework to buy for your school. These are sometimes known as a procurement framework or framework agreements.

Frameworks covers all spend levels. They are arrangements or buying rules that have been set up that helps you to buy specific goods and services compliantly, for example catering, cleaning and so on. The process you follow is usually guided and is shorter than running a procurement yourself.

Frameworks include criteria that must be fulfilled by the suppliers who have signed up to be a part of it, such as price, quality, aftercare and so on. This means you can trust that you’ll receive good quality goods or services that gives value for money.

Each framework will have a different list of suppliers:

    • Read about frameworks and what they are
    • Use the find a framework tool to find recommended procurement frameworks.

    Create a specification for your procurement

    A specification is the document you write to explain to suppliers what you want to buy.

    Use the create a specification tool to help you create a specification for:

    • catering
    • multifunctional devices or printers

    More categories will be added if schools find this tool useful.

    The tool will save you time by producing a specification based on the responses you give to a set of questions.

    The questions will cover essential information that you need to provide and automatically includes rules that suppliers must follow.

    You will need a DfE sign-in account to use it.

    Get specialist insurance for public sector schools

    The risk protection arrangement (RPA), is DfE's alternative to commercial insurance for public sector schools. Joining is easy and takes less than 5 minutes. Cover includes:

    • cyber
    • overseas and UK travel
    • public liability
    • material damage
