How to buy compliantly step by step

Once you know what you're buying and how much you're spending, you'll need to decide the best way to buy for your school based on the buying rules.

See our recommended buying routes to help you decide which route is best for you to follow.

Get free and impartial help from procurement specialists

Use the Get help buying for school service for advice, help and support from our team of DfE procurement specialists on buying goods and services.

Our procurement specialists can give advice at any stage of your procurement process.

This service applies to all state funded schools in England.

Use self-guided tools

You can use our tools at any time to help you at specific stages of your procurement. It's easier to use these tools if you're already familiar with the procurement process.

Use a DfE recommended buying solution

We recommend using a framework agreement to buy goods and services for your school. These are sometimes known as procurement frameworks.

A framework agreement is a set of buying rules that help you to buy specific goods and services compliantly, for example catering or cleaning.

You can use a framework agreement for any amount of spending. You'll follow a guided process and it's quicker and easier than running a procurement yourself.

Each framework has a list of suppliers who have signed up to be a part of it. The suppliers must meet criteria such as price, quality and aftercare. This means you can trust that you'll receive good quality goods or services that give value for money.

Use the find a framework tool to find recommended framework agreements. Once you've chosen a framework agreement, you'll be able to access its list of suppliers.

Create a specification for your procurement

A specification is a document you need to create to explain to suppliers exactly what you want to buy.

You can create one yourself or use the create a specification tool. The tool currently helps you create a specification for:

  • catering
  • multifunctional devices, a device that can copy, print, scan or fax

More categories will be added if schools find this tool useful.

The tool will save you time by producing a specification based on the responses you give to a set of questions.

The questions will cover essential information that you need to provide and automatically includes rules that suppliers must follow.

You will need a DfE sign-in account to use it.

Get specialist insurance for public sector schools

The risk protection arrangement (RPA), is DfE's alternative to commercial insurance for public sector schools. Joining is easy and takes less than 5 minutes. Cover includes:

  • cyber
  • overseas and UK travel
  • public liability
  • material damage
